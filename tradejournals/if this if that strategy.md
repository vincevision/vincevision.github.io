
##THIS IS A MULTITIMEFRAME ANALYSIS


---

Step-by-Step Trading Model

1️⃣ Determine Higher Timeframe Bias

Start with a higher timeframe (4H or 1H works well).

Mark:

Previous day high

Previous day low

Major swing highs

Major swing lows

Obvious supply and demand zones


You are asking one question:

Where is price most likely moving next?

Usually markets move toward liquidity.

Example:

Current price
↓
Next liquidity = previous high

So the narrative becomes:

> If price breaks this high → next target is the next liquidity/supply.




---

2️⃣ Identify the Key Level

Mark the level that must break to confirm continuation.

Examples:

previous high

previous low

equal highs

range high / range low


Example:

Resistance (key high)

This becomes your trigger level.


---

3️⃣ Wait for a Clean Break

Do NOT anticipate.

Let price actually break the level.

A good break usually shows:

• strong candle
• displacement
• momentum

Weak breaks often fail.

Better breakout:

Small candles
BIG impulse candle breaking the high

Weak breakout:

Slow drift above level

Those fail frequently.


---

4️⃣ Identify Displacement

After the break you want strong movement.

This shows real buyers or sellers entered.

Displacement often creates:

• Fair Value Gap (imbalance)
• strong momentum candles

Mark the FVG or origin of the move.

That is where you want price to return.


---

5️⃣ Wait for Retracement

Now you do nothing.

Let price pull back.

Common retracement areas:

• Fair Value Gap
• Demand / Supply zone
• 50% of displacement
• Previous structure

Price moves like this:

Expansion
↓
Retracement
↓
Continuation

You want to enter during retracement, not during expansion.


---

6️⃣ Drop to Lower Timeframe

Now move to 5m / 15m timeframe.

You are looking for one thing:

Market structure shift.

Before shift you usually see:

Lower highs
Lower lows

Then price breaks the last lower high.

Example:

LL
LH
LL
Break LH → shift

That tells you the retracement might be ending.


---

7️⃣ Entry

Your entry happens after the lower timeframe shift.

Example flow:

High breaks
↓
Displacement
↓
Retrace into FVG
↓
LTF structure shift
↓
Entry

You can enter on:

• small pullback after shift
• bullish candle confirmation
• retest of structure


---

8️⃣ Stop Loss

Place stop where the setup becomes invalid.

Common placements:

• below retracement low
• below FVG
• below liquidity sweep

Example:

Entry
Stop below retracement low

Never place stops randomly.


---

9️⃣ Take Profit

Targets should be liquidity zones.

Examples:

• next swing high
• equal highs
• supply zone
• previous day high

Market logic:

Liquidity → Liquidity

Price usually travels between these.


---

🔟 Risk Management

Even strong models lose trades.

Typical stats (approximate):

• Win rate: 40–60%
• Risk per trade: 0.5–1%
• Target: 1:2 or 1:3 RR

Example:

Risk 1%
Win 3%

You can lose several trades and still grow.


---

Full Model Summary

Your strategy simplified:

1 Identify HTF bias
2 Mark key high/low
3 Wait for break
4 Confirm displacement
5 Wait for retracement
6 Drop to LTF
7 Look for structure shift
8 Enter
9 Stop below invalidation
10 Target next liquidity


---

One Important Reality

This strategy works best in trending markets.

It performs worse during:

• choppy ranges
• low liquidity sessions
• news spikes

Filtering trades is critical.

You will discover:
